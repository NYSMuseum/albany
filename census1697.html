[The MS. has the following memorandum]: "List of the names & numbers of Inhabitants in ye City and County of Albany given me by ye Mayor of Albany, 31 July 1698."

The total number is not correctly given There were:

In the city of Albany........379 279 803
In Schenectady ............... 50   41 133
In other parts of the county......143   80  277

Exclusive of the fourteen negroes at Schenectady; therefore, according to the foregoing list, the total number of residents in the city and county of Albany: was men 379, women 270, children 803; making an aggregate of 1452 [?]



notes

This list, in other words - the census of 1697, is the most basic community based resource utilized by the Colonial Albany Project. This comprehensive survey document gave us a baseline population for establishing a population for early Albany. Simply stated, we identified these mainliners, their spouses, traced them back to their entry into the community, and identified their Albany descendants born thru 1800. Simple, eh!

The symbol "@" Denotes so-called ethnicity as attributed by the census taker. We surmise that the bracketed "F" stands for French; "I" for Irish; "E" for English; "Sp" for Spanish; and "Sc" for Scottish.

Rensselaerswyck: Census returns for the Manor printed in Annals, vol. 9, pp. 85-88. All the transcriptions printed in the Annals appear to be faithful copies of the manuscript in the State Archives. See "Finding the People."
