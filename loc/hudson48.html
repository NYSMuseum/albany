48 Hudson Avenue

Today, the Albany real property known as "48 Hudson Avenue" is the currently existing remains/shells of several buildings at that address dating at least into the first half of the eighteenth century. Today, this part of the historic community landscape is often referred to as "The Van Ostrande-Radliff House." As such, it has achieved considerable notoriety in the popular if not so historical record.

In 1813, the first published city directory identified Samuel Norton, a mason, as the occupant of 48 Hudson.

Today, 48 Hudson Avenue is owned by the Historic Albany Foundation - for many years, the most responsible steward of our city's built environment. Hopefully, HAF serves as its principal advocate now and for the foreseeable future.
