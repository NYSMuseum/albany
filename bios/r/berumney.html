Benjamin Rumney

by
Stefan Bielinski


Spelled and referred to variously, Benjamin Rumney lived in Albany during the second half of the eighteenth century. We seek information on his origins and path to Albany. However, he may have been a son of the first Jonathan and Johanna of Albany or the son of one of their children.

From 1744 thru 1759, six children were christened to Benjamin and Maria "Rumley" at the church in New Brunswick, New Jersey. One of the boys was baptized "Jonathan."

In June 1745, his name appeared in the accounts of an Albany area merchant.

In 1766 and '67, the house and property of Benjamin "Rumney/Romley in the third ward was assessed modestly. At that time, his house and lot in the West Manor also received a similar valuation. In May of 1767, "Ben Rumley" was identified as a private in a Rensselarswyck militia company.

In 1770, the pew at the Dutch church, formerly "owned" by the then deceased Bata Quackenbush, was transferred to "Benjamen Romblye."

After that, the name of Benjamin Rumney has not been found during our sweeps of community-based records or of Intenet-based resources. With so many outstanding questions still unresolved, we move on for now.




notes

Sources: The life of Benjamin Rumney hs not been assigned a CAP biography number. This sketch is derived chiefly from family and community-based resources.
