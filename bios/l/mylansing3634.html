Myndert Lansing

by
Stefan Bielinski


Myndert Lansing was born in January 1722. His parents were Abraham and Magdalena Van Tricht Lansing. He was the youngest son in the large family of a second ward mainstay. Both of his parents died in 1745 when Myndert was but twenty-three.

He is said to have graduated from Yale College in 1743 - and to have taken a second degree there in 1746. However, those accomplishments have yet to be substantiated.

A number of sources have identified "Myndert Lansing" as a trader in the West Indies in context with members of his immediate family and other merchant/importers.

But, by August 24, 1754, this Myndert Lansing was deceased as letters of administration were issued to his brother, Abraham Lansing of Albany. At that time, Myndert was a resident of Curacao. He had lived only thirty-two years.




notes

Sources: The life of Myndert Lansing is CAP biography number 3634. This sketch is derived chiefly from family and community-based resources.
