Jannetie Bradt Yates
by
Stefan Bielinski


Jannetie Bradt Yates was born is 1734. She was the daughter of Albany-area residents Daniel and Wyntie Bogardus Bradt.

She was past her twenty-seventh birthday when she married Albany native Abraham J. Yates in August 1761. Between 1763 and 1774, five of her children were baptized in the Albany Dutch church where she was a member and pewholder.

Wife of an Albany baker and city official, Jannetie raised her family in a modest home on one of Albany's back streets.

Abraham J. Yates filed his will in December 1794. It named his "beloved wife" as the beneficiary of all his estate during her widowhood. However, Jannetie Bradt Yates died in November 1804. Her husband was buried next to her three months later.
