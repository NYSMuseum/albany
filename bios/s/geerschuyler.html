Gertrude Schuyler Guest

by
Stefan Bielinski


Gertrude Schuyler was born in July 1775. She was the youngest daughter of Dirck and Mary Van Deusen Schuyler. Her Albany-born father was a one-time skipper and veteran of the War for Independence. Her mother was a native of New York City.

In March 1787, her deceased mother's children were considered (but not named) in the will filed by her maternal grandfather.

About 1793, Geertruy married newcomer Thomas Guest. Between 1795 and 1811, eight children had been christened at the Albany Dutch church.

In 1800, the couple was living in a modest house on Van Schaick Street.

We seek information on the later life and passing of Gertrude Schuyler Guest




notes

Sources: The life of Gertrude Schuyler Guest is CAP biography number 1317. This sketch is derived chiefly from family and community-based resources.

Demographics: Her children are listed in CSG (under #343 - in vol. II) but no particulars of her marriage to Guest are given. Her death date also does not appear.
