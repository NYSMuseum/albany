Cornelia Slingerland Evertsen

by
Stefan Bielinski


Cornelia Slingerland was born in December 1775. She was the daughter of Albany-area residents Albert and Christina Van Vranken Slingerland.

In August 1795, she married Hendrick Evertsen, a recent widower with children who was fourteen years older than his nineteen-year-old bride. By 1802, they added four more children to this Evertsen family.

Cornelia moved into Evertsen's home on Hudson Street where Hendrick practiced the wood-based trades of the carpenter and cooper.

Hendrick Evertsen died in July 1814 leaving Cornelia a thirty-nine-year-old widow. The next year, Cornelia Slingerland Evertsen was listed as the head of the household at 33 Hudson Street but not afterward. We seek information on her passing.




notes

Sources: The life of Cornelia Slingerland Evertsen is CAP biography number 5414. This sketch is derived chiefly from family and community-based resources.
