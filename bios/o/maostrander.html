Maria Ostrander Van Iveren

by
Stefan Bielinski


Maria Ostrander was was born in January 1715. She was an older daughter in the large family of Kingston native Johannes Ostrander and Albany-born Elizabeth Vandenbergh Ostrander. She may have grown up in her father's home in Albany's third ward.

In May 1740, she married Johannes Mynders Van Iveren at the Albany Dutch church. By 1756, eight children had been christened in Albany.

These Van Iverens lived in the first ward where Johannes practiced the blacksmith's trade.

Her husband filed a will in September 1761. It named Maria and three living children as his heirs. The will passed probate in January 1764.




notes

Sources: The life of Maria Ostrander Van Iveren has not been assigned a CAP biography number. This sketch is derived chiefly from family and community-based resources.
