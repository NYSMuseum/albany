Benjamin Joubert

by
Stefan Bielinski


Spelled variously, laborer Benjamin Joubert (also Jobart and Juba) lived in Albany during the early 1800s. He probably was born during the 1760s or early 1770s. He is said to have been from France.

From the 1810 census listing of his household, we think he may have been married.

In 1809, the third ward assessment roll valued his personal property on the Quay. He was called a "French laborer." His property was listed under the holdings of Barent and the late John R. Bleecker.

In 1810, the Albany census showed that his household had a young by, two adult males and two females, and four free persons of color.

In 1813, the first city directory listed him as a laborer at 32 Quay Street. After that, his name no longer appeared on the annual listing.





notes

Sources: The life of Benjamin Joubert has not been assigned a CAP biography number. This sketch is derived chiefly from family and community-based resources.
