Patrick Martin

by
Stefan Bielinski


Patrick Martin probably was born during the last quarter of the seventeenth century.

In March 1707, he married Mary Cox of Albany at the Albany Dutch church. At that time, he was identified as a drummer in Col. Ingoldsby's grenadiers. Their child was christened and he witnessed a baptism in July of that year.

After that, the name of Patrick Martin dropped from Albany rolls.




notes

Sources: The life of Patrick Martin has not been assigned a CAP biography number. This sketch is derived chiefly from family and community-based resources. In July 2008, an Internet search yielded only one source that was repeated in several contexts.
