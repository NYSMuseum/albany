Egbertie Egberts

by
Stefan Bielinski

Spelled and referenced variously, Egbertie Egberts was an inn and/or tavern keeper in Beverwyck during the 1650s and seems to have lived in Albany for many decades afterwards. We seek to ascertain her origins and trace her path to Albany.

Reference to an Egbertie Egberte as a tavern keeper. We seek to connect that pioneer with the individual further profiled below.

At this point, we have established "Egbertie" as the wife of New Netherland pioneer and Albany patriarch Teunis Teunise Egbertsen. Their union produced several children who became Albany residents.

In August 1685, Egbertie filed a joint will with her husband. It named all of their children. No probate information has been encountered.

We seek defining information on the later life and passing of the family matriarch known as "Egbertie Egberts."




notes

Sources: The life of Egbertie Egberts has no CAP biography number. This sketch is derived chiefly from family and community-based resources.
