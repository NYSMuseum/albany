The Albany Federal Committee - 1788


An Impartial Address to the Citizens of the City and County of Albany:
or
The 35 Anti-Federal Objections Refuted.
By the Federal Committee of the City of Albany [New York], April 1788

Text of Address

By Order of the Federal Committee,

ROBERT McCLALLEN, Chairman.

Members of the Committee:

STEPHEN LUSH
DANIEL HALE
THOMAS HUN
JOHN D.P. TEN EYCK
CORNELIUS GLEN
THEODORUS V.W. GRAHAM
JAMES CALDWELL
RICHARD SILL
PHILIP VAN RENSSELAER
LEONARD GANSEVOORT, jun.
JEREMIAH LANSINGH
STEPHEN VAN RENSSELAER
JAMES BLOODGOOD
THOMAS L. WITBEEK

notes

Selection copied from one presentation of an often-encountered online source. This list of the city's most prominent Federalists includes mostly younger and not-so-established (also newcomers) older community leaders. Conspicuously absent are General Schuyler, Abraham Ten Broeck, and the older Gansevoorts to name but a few. This presentation was intended to refute or at least rebuff a manifesto issued by prominent Albany Antifederalists in April.

Hun: Either Thomas D. or Thomas J. Hun, both of whom were born in 1736.
