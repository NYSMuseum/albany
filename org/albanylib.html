The Albany Library


In December 1791, a number of leading citizens formed an association to petition the State Legislature to incorporate the Albany Library.

An act was passed on February 24, 1792, incorporating the "trustees of the Albany Library." Abraham Ten Broeck, John Lansing, jr., Philip Schuyler. Stephen van Rensselaer, Jeremiah van Rensselaer, Thomas Ellison. John McDonald, James Fairlie, Daniel Hale, Hunloke Woodruff, Goldsbrow Banyar, and Stephen Lush made up the first Board of Trustees. Abraham Ten Broeck was named by the act the president of the association, and James Van Ingen was named treasurer and librarian.
